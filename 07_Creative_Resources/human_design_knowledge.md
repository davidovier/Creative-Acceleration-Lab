# Human-Centric Design Knowledge

## Universal Human Needs

### Core Needs (Design Anchors):

1. **Certainty**
   - Need to feel safe
   - Desire for predictability
   - Comfort and security
   - **Design implications**: Clear navigation, consistent patterns, safety signals

2. **Autonomy**
   - Need for control
   - Self-determination
   - Independence
   - **Design implications**: Choices, customization, opt-out options

3. **Connection**
   - Need for belonging
   - Relationships
   - Community
   - **Design implications**: Social features, shared experiences, communication

4. **Meaning**
   - Need for purpose
   - Significance
   - Impact
   - **Design implications**: Show impact, connect to larger purpose, storytelling

5. **Identity**
   - Need for self-expression
   - Recognition
   - Uniqueness
   - **Design implications**: Personalization, expression tools, identity affirmation

6. **Mastery**
   - Need for growth
   - Competence
   - Achievement
   - **Design implications**: Progressive challenges, skill-building, achievement systems

## Emotional Journey in Product Use

### 6 Stages:

1. **Awareness**
   - First encounter
   - Initial impression
   - Curiosity or resistance
   - **Design focus**: Clear value proposition, visual appeal, emotional hook

2. **Hope**
   - Belief in possibility
   - Optimism about outcome
   - Willingness to try
   - **Design focus**: Promise, social proof, quick wins

3. **Doubt**
   - Uncertainty emerges
   - Fear of failure
   - Question commitment
   - **Design focus**: Reassurance, support, evidence of progress

4. **Learning**
   - Engagement deepens
   - Skill development
   - Understanding grows
   - **Design focus**: Education, guidance, scaffolding

5. **Relief**
   - Problem solved
   - Tension released
   - Validation received
   - **Design focus**: Celebration, affirmation, clear outcomes

6. **Commitment**
   - Long-term adoption
   - Integration into life
   - Advocacy
   - **Design focus**: Deepening value, community, ongoing engagement

## Designing for Emotional Safety

### Principles:

1. **Remove Cognitive Overload**
   - Simplify choices
   - Progressive disclosure
   - Clear hierarchy
   - Minimal distraction

2. **Provide Clear Paths**
   - Obvious next steps
   - Visual wayfinding
   - Breadcrumbs and progress indicators
   - Escape routes

3. **Affirm User's Struggle**
   - Acknowledge difficulty
   - Normalize challenges
   - Show empathy in copy
   - Provide encouragement

4. **Reflect Their Worldview**
   - Use their language
   - Honor their values
   - Mirror their reality
   - Validate their experience

5. **Avoid Corporate Jargon Entirely**
   - Speak human
   - Use metaphor and story
   - Be direct and honest
   - Eliminate buzzwords

## Founder Psychology Insights

### Common Emotional Drivers:
- **Desire for autonomy** - freedom from constraints
- **Fear of irrelevance** - needing to matter
- **Longing for expression** - authentic voice
- **Panic caused by slowness** - urgency and momentum
- **Anger at bureaucracy** - system frustration
- **Need for identity clarity** - knowing who they are

### How to Extract True Motives:

**Prompts:**
- "What problem won't leave you alone?"
- "What truth do you wish someone told you earlier?"
- "When did you last feel fully alive?"
- "What felt unfair in your last job?"

### Behavioral Patterns to Recognize:
- **Overbuilding** → insecurity, need for validation
- **Underbuilding** → lack of clarity, fear of commitment
- **Avoiding confrontation** → unclear needs, conflict aversion
- **Endless pivots** → unexpressed emotional wound, lack of grounding

## Diagnostic Questionnaires

### Emotional Clarity Questionnaire:
- "What truth scares you?"
- "Where do you feel stuck in your body?"
- "What would you create if no one was watching?"
- "What are you pretending not to know?"
- "What permission do you need to give yourself?"

### Prototype Readiness Questionnaire:
- "What's the smallest version you'd accept?"
- "Who suffers if this isn't built?"
- "What's one thing this must do?"
- "What can you hardcode or fake?"
- "What would 'done' look like in 5 days?"
